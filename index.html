Suicide Statistics
∙ Worldwide, suicide rates are the highest they've been since World War II.
∙ In the last 45 years, suicide rates have increased by 60% worldwide.
∙ Close to 800,000 people die due to suicide every year, which is one person every 40 seconds.
∙ Suicide is the 10th leading cause of death worldwide.
∙ Suicide is a global phenomenon and occurs throughout the lifespan.
∙ There are indications that for each adult who died by suicide, there may have been more than 20 others attempting suicide.

What are ways to help?
We can all help prevent suicide.
If you know someone in crisis, call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (Lifeline) at 1-800-273-TALK (8255) or text the Criss Text Line (text HELLO to 741741). Both services are free and available 24 hours a day, seven days a week.
About this project:
∙ My name is Nicole Chan
∙ This was the final project for my undergraduate data visualization course at Boston College with Professor Kim.
∙ The World Health Organization has data on total deaths by suicide and suicide rates per 100,000 people overall and by age group and sex for 198 countries.
∙ This project seeks to inform the end user on worldwide suicide rates since 1985. I used an explanatory approach that combines data on suicides around the world with text displays that allow the user to fully immerse themselves with the visualizations and the gravity of what they are seeing. This data set gave the most complete look on an issue that is becoming more pressing as time goes on.
